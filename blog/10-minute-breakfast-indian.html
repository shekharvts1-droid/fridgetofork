Recipe Spotlight: Lightning-Fast Anda Bhurji (Masala Scrambled Eggs)

Here’s a perfect example of a delicious and protein-packed Indian breakfast that comes together in under 10 minutes. It's savory, spicy, and utterly satisfying!

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp oil or ghee
  • ¼ small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small green chili, finely chopped (optional, adjust to taste)
  • ¼ small tomato, finely chopped
  • Pinch of turmeric powder
  • Pinch of red chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a small non-stick pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onion and green chili. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until onions soften slightly.
  3. Stir in the chopped tomato. Cook for another minute until it softens.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder (if using), and salt. Mix well.
  5. Crack the eggs directly into the pan or whisk them in a bowl first.
  6. Scramble the eggs with a spatula, breaking them into small pieces as they cook. Cook for 2-3 minutes until they are set but still moist.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve immediately with a slice of toast or roti.

Smart Tips for Even Quicker Mornings

Want to shave off even more time? Here are some FridgeToFork pro tips:

  • Pre-chop Veggies: Chop onions, tomatoes, and chilies the night before and store them in an airtight container.
  • Spice Blends: Keep a pre-mixed "masala" blend (like a basic curry powder) ready for a quick flavor boost.
  • Batch Cook Garnish: Chop a larger batch of coriander and store it in the fridge.
  • Hot Water on Standby: For recipes like Upma, having a kettle of hot water ready drastically cuts down cooking time.
